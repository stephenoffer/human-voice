# Q3 Onboarding Review

Onboarding drives our retention numbers, and we have changed almost none of it in a year. The gap it opens is stark. Users who finish the first session retain at 64%. Users who stall before finishing retain at 19%. The flow handles the easy steps well, then abandons people at exactly the hard ones.

Self-serve activation cuts both ways. It dropped our support tickets per signup from 0.8 to 0.3. The cost is that a confused user now has no one to ask. We lose 22% of trials at the workspace-setup step. A quieter problem sits underneath: another 31% finish setup and never come back.

Account provisioning decides whether a trial converts, and right now it asks too much. A new admin connects each integration by hand, one at a time, in whatever order they happen to guess. We support 40 connectors with no recommended path through them.

Fix the setup step first. Week-one activation should rise enough to feed the upsell motion sales has wanted since spring.
