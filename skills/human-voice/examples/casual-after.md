# What a standing desk actually changed (and what it didn't)

I bought a standing desk in January, mostly because a coworker wouldn't shut up about hers. Six months in, here's what I'd tell my January self.

The first two weeks were rough. I went all-in on day one, stood for six hours straight, and could barely walk to the kitchen that night. Don't do that. Stand for an hour, sit for an hour, and let your legs catch up to the idea. By week three I stopped thinking about it.

Buy the mat before you buy the desk. I'm serious. The standing part isn't what wears you out, it's the floor. A cheap anti-fatigue mat fixed more discomfort than any amount of adjusting the desk height did.

Did it fix my back? Sort of. I move more now, shifting around instead of fossilizing in a chair, and that helps. But it didn't replace the actual problem, which was that I never went outside. The desk made me feel virtuous enough to skip the walk, which is exactly backwards.

Would I do it again? Yeah. Not because it's magic, but because standing made the bad afternoons less foggy. That's a small thing, and it was enough.
