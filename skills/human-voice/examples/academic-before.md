# Abstract

In today's digital age, the proliferation of online misinformation has become an increasingly important area of study. Studies suggest that exposure to misinformation may potentially influence political attitudes, and experts believe this could possibly have far-reaching consequences. This paper aims to explore the multifaceted landscape of correction strategies. It is widely known that fact-checking plays a vital role in combating false information. Our analysis determined that several approaches exist, each with its own tradeoffs and merits. We found that corrections might somewhat reduce belief in false claims under certain circumstances, although the effect could potentially vary. Ultimately, the best approach likely depends on a variety of factors and may warrant further research. This underscores the crucial importance of continued investigation in this ever-evolving field.
