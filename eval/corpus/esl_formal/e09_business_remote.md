# Why I am keeping the team remote

Several investors have asked me to bring the engineering team back to an office. I have decided not to, and I owe everyone a clear explanation.

We are 22 people across seven countries. When I founded the company in 2021, remote was a necessity because of the pandemic. Now it is a choice, and I make it with open eyes.

The honest argument against remote is real. New hires take longer to feel part of the group. Our last two junior engineers said they felt lonely in the first months, and we lost one of them partly for this reason. I take that seriously, and we now fly every new person to meet the team in person within their first three weeks.

But the argument for remote is stronger for us. Half my best engineers live in cities where we could never afford an office, and they would simply not work for me otherwise. Our retention is good. Last year only one person left, and not because of the location.

So we stay remote. We will spend more on travel, roughly 60 thousand dollars a year for quarterly gatherings, and we will invest in writing things down well. That is the deal, and I think it is the right one.
