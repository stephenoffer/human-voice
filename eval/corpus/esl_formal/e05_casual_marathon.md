# I finished my first marathon and it was harder than I thought

So last Sunday I ran the Berlin marathon. My time was 4 hours and 38 minutes. Not fast, I know. But I finished, and three months ago I could barely run 5 kilometres without stopping.

The first half felt almost easy. I had trained for four months, mostly early in the morning before work, and the cold air at kilometre 10 made me feel strong. Then kilometre 30 arrived. People warned me about the wall. I did not believe them. I should have believed them.

My legs became heavy like wood. A man next to me was walking and eating a banana, and for a moment I was jealous of that banana. I drank some cola at a station, which my coach had told me to do, and somehow it worked.

The last 2 kilometres I did not think about anything. Just the next tree, then the next one. When I saw the Brandenburg Gate I almost cried, and I am not a person who cries easily.

Will I do it again? Ask me next week. Today my knees say no.
