# A coffee roasted the way my grandfather did it

We are a small roastery in Trieste, and I want to tell you honestly why our coffee tastes different.

Most big brands roast fast and dark. It hides cheap beans. My grandfather never trusted that. He roasted slow, almost twelve minutes per batch, on a drum roaster from 1962 that still sits in our workshop and still complains when we start it in the morning.

We buy our green beans directly from two farms, one in Huila in Colombia and one in Sidamo. No middleman. We pay them more than the market price, because good beans are not free and the farmers should not be the ones who suffer for it.

The result is a cup that is bright but not sour. You will taste a little caramel, and at the end something like dried plum. If you only drink coffee to wake up, our beans are probably too expensive for you, and that is fine.

But if you like to sit with the cup for a few minutes and actually taste it, order our 250-gram bag. First order ships free inside Italy. Open it, smell it before you brew, and you will understand.
