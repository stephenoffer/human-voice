the literature on remote work productivity is a mess. honestly? half of it contradicts the other half, ngl.

early studies claimed big productivity gains. later replications shrank the effect. idk which to trust tbh.

the load-bearing variable everyone fights over is self-selection. people who choose remote may already be more productive, fr.

three camps exist in the field. optimists. skeptics. and the it-depends crowd.

the it-depends camp is giving the most honest read. context moderates everything. no notes.

a 2021 meta-analysis pooled 60 studies and found a small positive effect. that's growth, but the confidence interval is wide, lol.

publication bias is the elephant. null results don't get printed, smh, so the pooled estimate is probably inflated.

our contribution is a within-firm panel that sidesteps the selection problem. we track the same workers before and after a forced policy change.
