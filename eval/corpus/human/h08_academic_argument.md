# The replication crisis was a measurement crisis first

Psychology spent a decade calling it a replication crisis, as if the fix were simply running studies again with more subjects. That framing misses the deeper rot. Many of the famous failed effects were never measured well in the first place.

Take ego depletion. The original paradigm operationalized self-control as performance on a second tedious task after a first one. But the two tasks were never calibrated against each other, so a null result is impossible to interpret: did willpower not deplete, or was the second task simply easier? You cannot replicate your way out of an undefined construct.

The unglamorous work ahead is psychometric, not statistical. Before we argue about p-values, we have to agree on what we are counting. That requires building validated instruments, which earns no headlines and few grants, which is exactly why it keeps getting skipped.
