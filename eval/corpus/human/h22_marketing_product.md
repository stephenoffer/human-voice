# Notebooks that don't lose your work

You know the feeling. You're three hours into a notebook, the kernel dies, and the last save was somewhere around hour one. We built Cite for the version of you who has lived that afternoon.

Cite snapshots your notebook every time a cell runs. Not the output, the whole state: variables, imports, the half-broken function you were debugging. If the kernel crashes, you reopen and you're back where you were, down to the variable you assigned forty seconds ago.

It works with the Jupyter you already run. Install the extension, restart once, and it starts snapshotting in the background. You won't notice it until the day you need it.

We charge for teams, not individuals. If you're one person on your own machine, Cite is free, no account, no trial clock. If you're a lab or a company that wants shared snapshot history and access controls, that's the paid tier, and that's what keeps the free one running.

Try it on your messiest notebook. That's the one where it earns its keep.
