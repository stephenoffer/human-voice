# March numbers

We closed March at $214k in new bookings, which is short of the $240k target but better than I expected after the rough February we had. Two deals slipped into April because legal sat on the redlines for nine days. I've already flagged that to Dana.

The bigger story is churn. We lost three accounts, all of them small monthly plans under $500, and all three said the same thing: the onboarding took too long. One of them told me on a call that they signed up in January and didn't get their first dashboard until mid-February. That's on us.

Sales cycle length crept up to 41 days from 34 last quarter. I don't have a clean theory yet. Could be the new pricing page confusing people, could be noise. I'll pull the call recordings next week and see what comes out of them, because guessing hasn't gotten us anywhere and I'd rather hear it from the customers than invent a reason that flatters us.

Cash is fine through Q3.

We don't need to panic about the bookings miss, but we do need to fix onboarding before it costs us another quarter. Dana and I are meeting Thursday to scope it.
