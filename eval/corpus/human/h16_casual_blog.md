# I tried to bike to work for a month

My commute is 6.2 miles and I figured, how hard can it be.

Turns out the hard part is not the riding. It's showing up to a 9am meeting having sweat through your shirt in July, then sitting in a glass conference room while your colleagues politely pretend not to notice. First week I gave up twice and drove. Pine Street has a hill that's short but mean, and the first time I hit it I had to walk the bike up like a kid, which I will not be putting on my resume.

By week three I could ride that hill without my legs filing a complaint, and it felt like an actual accomplishment.

What nobody warned me about is how much you notice from a bike. I found a bakery two blocks off my old driving route that I'd passed a hundred times in the car and never once seen. They make a cardamom bun that has genuinely changed my mornings.

Did I keep it up? Mostly. I bike maybe four days a week now. On the rainy ones I drive and feel a little guilty, then I get over it by lunch.
