# I finally learned to fry an egg

For thirty-four years I made bad fried eggs and assumed that was just how eggs were. Rubbery whites, a yolk cooked to chalk, the edges gone brown and bitter. I thought people who liked fried eggs were lying.

Then my friend Dao made me one in her tiny kitchen in about ninety seconds, and it was a completely different food. Soft, glossy white. Yolk that ran. No brown anywhere. I sat there a little betrayed by every egg that had come before.

The whole secret, it turns out, is that I had the heat way too high the entire time. I was cooking eggs like I was searing a steak. Dao keeps the pan at barely a medium, slides the egg in, and the moment the white sets she's already moving it to the plate. The yolk finishes from residual heat.

I've made one almost every morning since. Some mornings I still rush it and the yolk seizes up, and I eat it anyway and feel a little ashamed. But most mornings now it comes out right, and there's something stupidly satisfying about getting good at a thing you'd written off as impossible. It was never the eggs.
