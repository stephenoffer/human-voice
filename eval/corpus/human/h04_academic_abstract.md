# Tidal heating limits on Proxima b's habitability

We model tidal dissipation in the rocky interior of Proxima Centauri b under a range of orbital eccentricities from 0.0 to 0.1. For eccentricities above 0.06, internal heating exceeds 2 W/m^2, comparable to Io, which would drive runaway volcanism incompatible with stable surface liquid water.

The eccentricity is poorly constrained by current radial-velocity data. Our point here is narrow: habitability arguments that assume a circularized orbit are doing a lot of unstated work. If the eccentricity is even moderate, the planet's surface is governed by tidal heat, not stellar flux.

We do not claim Proxima b is uninhabitable. We claim the question cannot be answered until the eccentricity is measured to better than 0.02, which the upcoming RV campaign should achieve by 2027.
