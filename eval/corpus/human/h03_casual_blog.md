# I made sourdough for a year and here's what nobody tells you

The starter is not a pet. You will read forty blog posts that treat it like a fragile creature with feelings. It is flour and water and some yeast that wants to eat. I left mine in the fridge for three weeks once, fed it twice, and it came roaring back the next day.

What actually matters is the dough temperature, and almost nobody mentions it. Cold kitchen, slow rise, sour loaf. Warm kitchen, fast rise, mild loaf. I bought a $9 thermometer and my bread stopped being a coin flip.

The crumb shots on Instagram are mostly lies, by the way. Half of them are underbaked. A loaf that holds its shape and sounds hollow when you knock it is a good loaf. Stop chasing the holes.
