# Proposal: move warehouse fulfillment in-house

Right now we pay ShipBob $3.10 per order plus storage. At 8,000 orders a month that's roughly $25k, and it climbs every time we run a promo. I think we should bring fulfillment in-house by August.

The math: a 4,000 square foot space near the Oakland port runs about $7k a month. Two part-time packers at $22/hour cover our current volume with room to spare. Add shelving, a label printer, and the first pallet of boxes and we're looking at maybe $18k upfront. We'd break even around month five.

The real win isn't the money, though it helps. It's control.

Last Black Friday ShipBob held our inventory for four days because their dock was slammed, and we ate 200 refund requests, plus a wave of angry tweets that Marketing spent a week mopping up. If that warehouse is ours and a pallet shows up late, at least it's our fire to put out instead of waiting on someone else's dock manager to call us back.

The risk is obvious: we've never run a warehouse and I don't want to learn during peak season. So my plan is to start small in July, run both systems in parallel for a month, and only cut over once we've shipped a few thousand orders without a mess.
